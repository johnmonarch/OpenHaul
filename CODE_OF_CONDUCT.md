# Code of Conduct

OpenHaul Guard contributors are expected to keep project spaces professional, specific, and useful.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of other contributors and users.
- Discuss technical disagreements with evidence, examples, and clear tradeoffs.
- Keep issue and pull request comments focused on the work.
- Assume sensitive operational data may be present and ask reporters to sanitize it.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope, priority, release timing, and repository hygiene.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing another person's private information.
- Posting secrets, private carrier packet contents, customer data, or exploit details in public issues.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or pull requests after maintainers have redirected the discussion.
- Spam, impersonation, or other behavior that disrupts project maintenance.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or delete comments; close or lock issues; decline contributions; or block participants when needed to protect the project and its users.

If you need to report conduct concerns, contact the maintainers privately. Include links, screenshots, timestamps, and a short summary of the concern. Do not use public issues for private safety or security reports.
